Frequently Asked Questions

The Very Basics

What is a Blockchain?

Blockchain is a distributed and decentralized digital ledger that stores all kinds of data, shared among a whole network of computers, instead of being managed by one centralized system. Recording information in this way ensures that the system is safe and it is very difficult to hack or edit, reducing the risk for people using it.

What is a Smart Contract?

A smart contract is a self-executing contract that runs on a blockchain, essentially it is a digital version of the standard paper contract. When you sign a contract it executes automatically once the terms are met from both sides. Smart contracts are used to automate all kinds of financial transactions in a safe and affordable way.

What is minting?

Minting is the process of converting a digital file into a digital asset stored on the blockchain. Once the NFT is minted it is available for buying and selling.

What is an NFT?

NFT stands for "non-fungible token", which means that it is unique and can’t be replicated, and each can have only one owner. NFTs are stored on a blockchain and can be used to collect all kinds of digital art, videos, music, and much more.

Does Cardano have NFTs?

Yes, Cardano does have NFTs! You can explore projects on our marketplace and find links to join their communities.

What is a Policy ID?

Think of it as a serial number for a collection. Only valid NFTs from that collection will have that particular Policy ID. This is the easiest way to avoid buying fake NFTs (check their policy ID vs the ID of the verified collection).

What is a UTXO?

The method Cardano uses for transactions. You can find a more in-depth technical answer here

Why Cardano?

Cardano is an open platform that seeks to provide economic identity to the billions who lack it by providing decentralized applications to manage identity, value, and governance.

Wallets

Can I use a hardware wallet?

Yes! You can use a hardware wallet, but at the moment the only one that fully supports smart contracts is Eternl.

What Cardano wallets can I use?

Plenty of wallets support ADA, so it is not always easy to choose! However, to narrow down your options, on JPG Store, there are 6 different wallets supported - Nami, Eternl, Flint, Gero, and Typhon and NuFi.

How do you create a Cardano wallet?

To create a wallet, you will have to get a browser extension. The wallet must be compatible with your browser, so don’t forget to check that first! After adding the browser extension, you will be guided through the setup process.
You can find a full tutorial to install Eternl wallet here.

How do you send / receive ADA with my wallet?

First, make sure your wallet is all set up! When creating a wallet, you will have your wallet address, a seed phrase, and a password.
Other people can send funds to your wallet address, as you can send ADA to others. It is similar to when you need to know someone’s email address to send them an email.

What is Collateral?

Just a small amount of ADA (usually 5) is set aside to help against malicious attacks, allowing the wallet to interact with smart contracts. You can get it back at any time by turning off the collateral.

How do you set collateral?

Varies by the wallet. Generally in the main settings. (you will have to sign a transaction to complete)
